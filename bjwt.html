about

The Black Jaguar White Tiger Foundation is dedicated to rescuing animals from abusive or bad circumstances such as breeding, circuses and pets. The Foundation cares for over 210 large felines including leopards, lynxes, pumas, lions and tigers. They work closely with the Mexican government to negotiate the surrender of animals from circuses and breeders. One of the main goals of the Foundation is to reproduce, reintroduce and repopulate endangered species of Mexican birds into their natural habitat.

mission statement

There mission statement is rooted in four principles.
1. To rescue as many animals in need as possible.
2. To raise awareness worldwide through social media and conferences.
3. To continue to work with the Mexican government to change laws to protect more species both locally and globally.
4. To progress with the Endangered Bird Project and advance the project to include felines native to Mexico like jaguars, ocelots, oncillas and jaguarundis.
